The NNF Center at Broad is dedicated to overcoming the "variant-to-function" bottleneck in metabolic research. We are developing the infrastructure to prosecute genes to mechanisms at scale, serving as a model for all common complex diseases.

Our Core Pillars:
 - **Flagship Disease Projects (FDP)**: A systematic effort to map the genes, cell types, and pathways driving T2D and obesity to pinpoint novel targets for prevention and therapy.
 - **Human Gene Regulation Map (HGRM)**: Deciphering the regulatory code of metabolically relevant cells and tissues to predict the functional impact of risk variants.
 - **Advanced Data Science**: Leveraging breakthroughs in ML and AI to synthesize vast genomic datasets into actionable therapeutic hypotheses.

**Our Goal**: To move from genetic discovery to precision medicine, ensuring the right treatment reaches the right patient subgroup.
